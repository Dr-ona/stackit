# Stackit FAQ

## Getting started

### Does Stackit work offline?

Yes. Dictionary lookup, saving words, browsing your Library, and reviewing with
spaced repetition all work without an internet connection. Cloud sync, AI-powered
meaning discovery, and account management require internet access.

### What languages does Stackit support?

English, Arabic, and French. All nine route combinations work offline:
EN↔AR, EN↔FR, and AR↔FR (through English pivot), plus same-language study
(e.g., English words studied with English definitions).

### How do I capture a word from another app?

Select text in any app that supports Android text selection (browser, e-reader,
news app, etc.), then tap **Understand with Stackit** from the action menu. You
can also add words manually from the capture screen, or paste from clipboard.

### What does the "Word" or "2 words" badge mean?

When you capture text, Stackit shows a small badge indicating whether the
selection is a single word or a multiword expression (phrase). This helps you
know whether the dictionary will look up the whole expression or just one word.

### Does Stackit capture the sentence around my selection?

When the source app provides it, Stackit captures the surrounding sentence
as context. This context is used to rank dictionary senses by relevance to
your original sentence. The context is stored locally and never leaves your
device unless you sign in.

### What app information is stored with each capture?

Each capture may store the source app name, a URL if one was found in the
selected text, and a capture timestamp. This metadata helps you remember
where a word came from. By default, source app, URL, and surrounding sentence
are stored **locally only**. You can enable a consent toggle on each entry to
sync this metadata to the cloud with your vocabulary.

## Vocabulary and meaning

### What does "Find all meanings" do?

Tap **Find all meanings** on any saved entry to let Gemini expand it into
distinct senses with IPA pronunciation, transliteration, gender, inflections,
register labels (formal, informal, technical, etc.), and semantic relations
(synonyms, antonyms, collocations, idioms).

### Where do IPA, gender, and inflections come from?

They are generated by AI when you tap "Find all meanings." They are not stored
if AI features are disabled.

### Can I choose which senses to save?

Yes. When an entry has multiple senses, checkboxes appear on the capture preview
screen. Uncheck any sense you do not want before saving.

### What does the provenance badge mean?

Each sense shows whether its meaning came from the **offline dictionary**, was
generated by **Gemini AI**, or was entered **manually** by you. Tap the badge
icon in the detail view to see the source.

### How do I report an incorrect meaning?

Open the entry detail view, tap the three-dot menu on any sense card, and
choose a reason (wrong meaning, outdated definition, offensive content, or
other issue). Reports are logged for review — your vocabulary is not affected.

### Can I see why a word is being reviewed?

Yes. The due-reason explanation appears at the top of each review card,
telling you whether the card is new, overdue, weak, or due for maintenance.

## Review and exercises

### How does the review scheduler work?

Stackit uses **FSRS** (Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler), an adaptive model
that adjusts review intervals for each sense individually based on your actual
recall history. Senses you remember well get longer intervals; ones you struggle
with come back sooner.

### What exercise types are available?

- **Cloze** — fill in the blank in a sentence
- **Multiple choice** — pick the correct translation or definition
- **Reverse translation** — translate from target to source language
- **Definition match** — match a sense to its correct definition

### What are exercise modes?

- **New words** — senses you have not reviewed yet
- **Weak meanings** — senses with low stability or high error rates
- **Overdue review** — senses past their scheduled review date
- **Mixed** — balanced combination of all the above

### I cannot use audio or speech. Can I still review?

Yes. Enable **Text-only mode** in exercise settings. All exercise types work
with typed input only — no audio or speech required.

### What is "weak-meaning detection"?

Stackit tracks how well you remember each individual sense using FSRS signals
and error counts. Senses that are frequently forgotten or have low stability
are flagged as weak and prioritized in weak-meaning exercise sessions.

## Library and organization

### How do I create a collection?

From any entry's detail view, library tile, or after saving a capture, tap the
**Add to Collection** button. You can select an existing collection or create a
new one inline.

### Can one word belong to multiple collections?

Yes. A vocabulary entry can belong to zero, one, or multiple collections without
duplicating its meanings or review history.

### Can I change the accent for pronunciation?

Yes. Go to Profile > Learning languages, tap a language, and choose an accent
from the dropdown. Available accents include US, British, Australian, and
Indian English; Saudi, Egyptian, Moroccan, and Emirati Arabic; and France,
Canada, and Belgian French. The accent applies when you tap the speaker icon
on any entry.

### How do I undo a delete?

When you swipe-to-delete an entry in the Inbox or use bulk delete in the
Library, a brief undo SnackBar appears at the bottom of the screen. Tap
**Undo** to restore the deleted entry before it disappears. You can also
restore by using the "Undo" option in the review screen.

### How do I select multiple entries?

Long-press any entry in the Library to enter multi-select mode. Tap additional
entries to select them, then use the bulk action bar to add to collections,
toggle favorites, or delete selected entries.

### Can I delete a collection without deleting the words?

Yes. In the collection picker, tap the delete icon next to a collection name.
This removes the collection and unlinks its entries, but the vocabulary itself
is never deleted.

## Data and privacy

### What happens to my data?

All vocabulary, review history, collections, tags, and preferences are stored
locally on your device. When you sign in, this data is synced to your private
Firebase account. Source app name, URL, and surrounding sentence are stored
locally by default and are only synced to the cloud when you explicitly enable
the consent toggle for each entry. Your data is never used for advertising or
sold to third parties.

### Can I export my vocabulary?

Yes. Open Profile > Export to download your vocabulary as JSON, CSV, or
Anki-compatible file at any time. You can also import from JSON or CSV.

### Can I delete my data?

Yes. You can delete individual entries from the Library, or permanently delete
your entire account and cloud data from Account & settings.

### When is AI used?

AI is only used when you explicitly request it:
- **Find all meanings** — expand a word into full senses
- **Explain in context** — explain a word in its original sentence
- **Bilingual examples** — auto-generate examples when saving a sense that
  lacks them

AI can be disabled entirely in your profile settings.

### What analytics are collected?

If you opt in to product analytics, anonymous events are logged (first capture,
first save, session completion, return timestamps). These events contain
**no vocabulary text and no personal information**. Analytics can be disabled
at any time.

## Account

### How do I create an account?

Tap Profile > Sign in. You can use email/password or Google Sign-In.

### What if I lose my device?

Sign in on your new device with the same account. Your vocabulary, review
history, and profile will sync from the cloud.

### How do I check my sync status?

Go to Account & settings. You'll see the last synced time and how many entries
are in your cloud vocabulary. Tap **Sync now** to force an immediate sync if
needed.

### How do I delete my account?

Go to Account & settings > Delete account and cloud data. This permanently
removes your Firebase account, cloud vocabulary, and profile. Local data on
your device is also cleared.

## Troubleshooting

### Stackit does not appear in the text selection menu

Make sure Stackit is installed and enabled in your device's text selection
settings. On some devices, you may need to enable it under Settings > Apps >
Default apps > Assist app, or in the text selection action menu configuration.
As an alternative, you can paste text directly from clipboard using the
**Paste from clipboard** button on the inbox screen.

### My offline dictionary is not finding a word

The offline dictionary covers common vocabulary from FreeDict. Rare or
very recent words may not be included. Try tapping **Find all meanings** to
let Gemini look up additional senses.

### Review reminders are not showing

Check that notifications are enabled for Stackit in your device settings.
You can also adjust reminder time from your profile settings.
